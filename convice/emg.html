EMG & NCS

EMG (Electromyography) & NCS (Nerve Conduction Study)

EMG is a diagnostic test that measures the electrical activity of muscles. It involves inserting very fine needles (electrodes) into specific muscles to record the electrical signals produced when the muscles contract and relax. These signals, called motor unit action potentials, provide information about the health and function of the muscles and the nerves controlling them.

Uses of EMG:
  • Diagnosing and differentiating between muscle disorders, such as muscular dystrophy, myasthenia gravis, and myopathies.
  • Identifying the source of muscle weakness or atrophy.
  • Assessing nerve damage in conditions like peripheral neuropathy.
  • Evaluating conditions that affect the connection between nerves and muscles, such as nerve injuries or motor neuron diseases.

NCS (Nerve Conduction Study): NCS is a test that measures the speed and strength of nerve signals as they travel along peripheral nerves. It involves applying small electrical shocks to the skin's surface and recording the nerve's response. The electrical signals, or nerve conduction velocities, provide information about the health and function of the nerves.

Uses of NCS:
  • Diagnosing and differentiating between nerve-related conditions, such as carpal tunnel syndrome, neuropathies, and nerve injuries.
  • Assessing the extent and severity of nerve damage.
  • Identifying the location of nerve compression or entrapment.

EMG and NCS Combined: EMG and NCS are often performed together in a single session, known as an EMG/NCS study. This combined approach provides a comprehensive assessment of the neuromuscular system. During the study, the healthcare provider may test multiple muscles and nerves in different areas of the body, depending on the individual's symptoms and suspected conditions.

Procedure and Considerations: The procedures are generally well-tolerated, and any discomfort is usually brief and minimal. EMG involves inserting needles into muscles, which might cause mild discomfort. NCS involves applying small electrical shocks, which can feel like a brief, tingling sensation. These tests can provide valuable information about the underlying causes of muscle weakness, pain, numbness, or other neuromuscular symptoms.
